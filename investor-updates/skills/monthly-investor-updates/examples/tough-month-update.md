# Example: Tough Month Update

A sample investor update when challenges outweigh highlights.

---

**Subject: Acme Corp Monthly Update - March 2024**

Hi everyone,

**TL;DR:**
- Lost our largest customer (Globex) - $150K ARR impact
- ARR down to $1.9M (-8% MoM)
- Taking immediate action on retention and diversification

---

## What Happened

Globex, our largest customer at $150K ACV, churned this month. They were acquired by a competitor who uses a competing solution. We had 30 days notice and explored all options, but the decision was made at acquirer level.

Additionally, two SMB customers churned due to budget cuts ($15K combined).

---

## Honest Assessment

This hurts, and it exposed a concentration risk we should have addressed sooner. At their peak, Globex was 12% of our ARR - too high for a single customer.

What we got right:
- Product wasn't the issue; Globex was a happy customer until acquisition
- Our retention process worked - we escalated appropriately

What we need to improve:
- Customer concentration limits
- Earlier M&A intelligence
- Expansion in existing accounts to diversify

---

## Our Response

**Immediate (this month):**
1. Reached out to all customers >5% of ARR - confirmed health
2. Accelerated 3 pipeline deals - closed 1 already ($40K)
3. Reduced discretionary spend by $15K/month

**Next 60 days:**
4. Launch customer health scoring to catch at-risk accounts earlier
5. Push expansion playbook - target 3 upsells in existing base
6. Set policy: no single customer >8% of ARR going forward

---

*Why this works:*
- Doesn't hide the bad news - leads with it
- Takes accountability without being defensive
- Shows clear action plan with specifics
- Includes silver linings without being dismissive
- Asks are relevant to the challenge
- Offers to discuss further
- Maintains professional confidence
