# Company Winddown Checklist

## Pre-Decision Phase

Before deciding to wind down, ensure you've considered:

- [ ] All alternative funding options exhausted
- [ ] Pivot options evaluated
- [ ] Acqui-hire possibilities explored
- [ ] Asset sale opportunities assessed
- [ ] Board alignment on decision
- [ ] Legal counsel consulted

## Legal and Governance

### Board and Investors

- [ ] Hold formal board meeting to approve winddown
- [ ] Document the decision and rationale in board minutes
- [ ] Notify all investors in writing
- [ ] Understand fiduciary duties (they shift to creditors if insolvent)
- [ ] Determine if any investor consents needed
- [ ] Review shareholder agreements for winddown provisions

### Corporate Legal

- [ ] Engage experienced winddown counsel
- [ ] Determine appropriate winddown structure (dissolution vs ABC vs bankruptcy)
- [ ] Review all contracts for termination provisions
- [ ] Identify contracts with assignment restrictions
- [ ] Review and address any pending litigation
- [ ] Understand director/officer liability protection

### Regulatory

- [ ] Notify relevant regulatory bodies (if applicable)
- [ ] Understand data privacy obligations (GDPR, CCPA)
- [ ] File any required government notifications
- [ ] Understand industry-specific requirements

## Financial

### Creditor Analysis

- [ ] List all creditors and amounts owed
- [ ] Prioritize creditors (secured, priority, unsecured)
- [ ] Calculate total liabilities
- [ ] Compare assets to liabilities (solvent vs insolvent)

**Creditor priority order:**
1. Secured creditors (with collateral)
2. Priority claims (taxes, wages, ERISA)
3. Unsecured creditors (vendors, etc.)
4. Preferred stockholders
5. Common stockholders

### Asset Inventory

- [ ] Cash and bank accounts
- [ ] Accounts receivable
- [ ] Intellectual property
- [ ] Equipment and furniture
- [ ] Deposits and prepaid expenses
- [ ] Customer contracts/relationships
- [ ] Domain names, trademarks

### Final Financial Actions

- [ ] Close unnecessary bank accounts (keep one for winddown)
- [ ] Stop automatic payments
- [ ] Collect outstanding receivables
- [ ] Calculate final payroll and obligations
- [ ] Reserve for winddown expenses
- [ ] Prepare final financial statements

## Employment

### Legal Requirements

- [ ] Review WARN Act requirements (60-day notice if 100+ employees)
- [ ] Calculate all final pay obligations
- [ ] Verify PTO payout requirements by state
- [ ] Prepare separation agreements if offering severance
- [ ] Ensure final paychecks meet state deadlines
- [ ] Prepare COBRA notices

### Final Pay

| State | Final Pay Deadline (Termination) |
|-------|----------------------------------|
| California | Same day |
| New York | Next payday |
| Texas | Within 6 days |
| [Your state] | [Check requirement] |

### Severance Considerations

- [ ] Determine severance budget
- [ ] Create consistent severance formula
- [ ] Prepare release agreements
- [ ] Consider ADEA requirements (if 40+ employees affected)
- [ ] Allow proper consideration period for releases

### Benefits

- [ ] Notify health insurance carrier
- [ ] Prepare COBRA election notices
- [ ] Handle 401(k) plan termination (requires process)
- [ ] Address unvested equity
- [ ] Process final expense reimbursements

### Communication

- [ ] Prepare employee notification (see templates below)
- [ ] Prepare FAQ document
- [ ] Identify spokesperson for questions
- [ ] Plan announcement logistics
- [ ] Prepare reference policy
- [ ] Offer to write LinkedIn recommendations

## Customers and Partners

### Customer Notification

- [ ] Identify all active customers
- [ ] Prioritize by impact and relationship
- [ ] Prepare customer communication
- [ ] Plan data export/migration support
- [ ] Provide transition timeline
- [ ] Honor refund obligations

### Customer Data

- [ ] Understand data obligations per contracts
- [ ] Plan data return or deletion process
- [ ] Provide adequate time for customer data export
- [ ] Document data handling for compliance

### Partners and Vendors

- [ ] List all active contracts
- [ ] Review termination provisions
- [ ] Send termination notices per contract terms
- [ ] Negotiate early termination where possible
- [ ] Return any partner property/assets

## Operations

### Office and Facilities

- [ ] Review lease termination provisions
- [ ] Negotiate early termination or sublease
- [ ] Plan office cleanout
- [ ] Return keys, badges, parking passes
- [ ] Cancel utilities and services
- [ ] Handle mail forwarding

### Technology and Systems

- [ ] Backup all critical data
- [ ] Determine what to preserve vs delete
- [ ] Cancel SaaS subscriptions
- [ ] Shut down infrastructure
- [ ] Handle domain name disposition
- [ ] Close social media accounts (or transfer)

### Equipment

- [ ] Inventory all company equipment
- [ ] Collect equipment from employees
- [ ] Decide: sell, donate, or dispose
- [ ] Handle leased equipment returns

## Intellectual Property

### IP Disposition

- [ ] Inventory all IP (patents, trademarks, copyrights, trade secrets)
- [ ] Determine value and potential buyers
- [ ] Consider selling IP to extend returns
- [ ] Handle IP assignment from employees
- [ ] Abandon or sell unused trademarks

### Code and Data

- [ ] Backup all source code
- [ ] Determine if code can be sold or open-sourced
- [ ] Handle customer data per obligations
- [ ] Destroy confidential business data appropriately

## Tax and Accounting

### Tax Obligations

- [ ] File final federal tax return
- [ ] File final state tax return(s)
- [ ] Pay all payroll taxes (FICA, state withholding)
- [ ] File final payroll tax returns
- [ ] Handle any outstanding sales tax
- [ ] Get tax clearance certificate if required by state

### Final Accounting

- [ ] Prepare final financial statements
- [ ] Calculate and process final distributions (if any)
- [ ] Provide K-1s or other investor documentation
- [ ] Retain records per legal requirements (7 years typically)

## Corporate Dissolution

### State Filings

- [ ] File certificate of dissolution with state
- [ ] Publish notice if required by state
- [ ] File final annual report
- [ ] Cancel business licenses
- [ ] Cancel DBA registrations

### Federal Filings

- [ ] File final IRS return (Form 966 for corporations)
- [ ] Close EIN (Letter 147C or form request)

### Final Steps

- [ ] Distribute remaining assets per priority
- [ ] Close bank accounts
- [ ] Retain records securely
- [ ] Destroy company stamps/seals
- [ ] Archive critical documents

## Communication Templates

### Employee Announcement

> Team,
>
> I have difficult news to share. After careful consideration, we've made the decision to wind down [Company].
>
> I know this isn't the news any of us wanted. [Brief explanation of why].
>
> Here's what this means for you:
> - **Last day of employment:** [Date]
> - **Severance:** [Details]
> - **Benefits:** [COBRA information]
> - **Final pay:** [When and how]
>
> We'll be providing [support services]. I'm committed to helping each of you land well.
>
> I'm deeply grateful for everything you've contributed. This outcome doesn't reflect your work or commitment.
>
> [Next steps and availability for questions]
>
> [Name]

### Customer Notification

> Dear [Customer],
>
> I'm writing with important news about [Company]. We've made the difficult decision to wind down operations, with service ending on [Date].
>
> We're grateful for your business and want to ensure a smooth transition.
>
> **What you need to know:**
> - Service continues until: [Date]
> - Your data: [How to export, timeline]
> - Refunds: [Policy for any prepaid services]
> - Alternative solutions: [If you can recommend any]
>
> If you have questions, please contact [email/person] by [date].
>
> Thank you for being part of our journey.
>
> [Name]

### Investor Notification

> Dear [Investor],
>
> I'm writing with difficult news. After exhausting alternatives, we've decided to wind down [Company].
>
> **Situation:**
> [Brief explanation of why - runway, market, etc.]
>
> **What we explored:**
> [Alternatives considered - bridge, pivot, sale]
>
> **The plan:**
> - Wind down timeline: [Dates]
> - Outstanding obligations: [Summary]
> - Expected return of capital: [Honest assessment]
>
> **Next steps:**
> [What you need from them, if anything]
>
> I take responsibility for this outcome. I'm committed to handling the wind down professionally and will keep you informed throughout the process.
>
> I'm available to discuss.
>
> [Name]
