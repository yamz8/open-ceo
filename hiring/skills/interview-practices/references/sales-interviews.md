# Sales Interview Guide

## Interview Structure for Sales Roles

### Recommended Process

| Round | Duration | Focus | Interviewer |
|-------|----------|-------|-------------|
| Phone Screen | 30 min | Background, motivation, logistics | Recruiter |
| Hiring Manager | 45 min | Experience, sales methodology | Sales leader |
| Role Play | 45-60 min | Discovery, pitch, objection handling | Senior AE or manager |
| Team/Culture | 30-45 min | Collaboration, values | Team members |
| Executive | 30 min | Final assessment, close | Founder or exec |

### Adjustments by Role

**SDR:** Focus on energy, coachability, resilience
**AE:** Full process with role play emphasis
**Sales Manager:** Add leadership scenarios, team building questions
**VP/CRO:** Add strategy, executive presentation, reference calls

## SDR Interviews

### What to Assess

| Competency | What to Look For |
|------------|------------------|
| Energy & Drive | Enthusiasm, work ethic, hunger |
| Coachability | Takes feedback, adapts approach |
| Resilience | Handles rejection, bounces back |
| Communication | Clear, engaging, professional |
| Organization | Can manage volume, prioritize |

### SDR Interview Questions

**Phone Screen:**
- What interests you about sales and this role specifically?
- Walk me through your day yesterday - how did you spend your time?
- Tell me about a time you had to be persistent to achieve something.

**Hiring Manager:**
- What do you know about our company and product?
- How do you handle rejection?
- Tell me about a goal you set and achieved through effort.
- What's your approach to time management and prioritization?

**Role Play - Cold Call Simulation:**
- Give candidate company context and target persona
- Have them "cold call" you as a prospect
- Assess: confidence, value prop clarity, handling of objections
- Give feedback mid-call to assess coachability

### SDR Red Flags
- No research on the company
- Can't handle mild rejection in role play
- Defensive when receiving feedback
- Unclear career motivation

## Account Executive Interviews

### What to Assess

| Competency | What to Look For |
|------------|------------------|
| Discovery Skills | Asks great questions, uncovers pain |
| Business Acumen | Understands business problems, ROI |
| Deal Management | Navigates complex sales, multi-stakeholder |
| Closing | Confident ask, handles objections |
| Process & Method | Structured approach, CRM discipline |

### AE Interview Questions

**Experience Deep-Dive:**
- Walk me through your biggest deal. Start from how you found it.
- What's your sales process? How do you qualify opportunities?
- Tell me about a deal you lost. What happened and what did you learn?
- How do you prepare for a first meeting with a new prospect?
- Describe your relationship with your sales manager. How do you work together?

**Sales Methodology:**
- How do you identify the economic buyer?
- What questions do you ask in a discovery call?
- How do you handle the "we're happy with our current solution" objection?
- Walk me through how you build a business case for your solution.

### AE Role Play - Discovery Call

**Setup:**
- Provide company context (your product, target customer)
- Give candidate a scenario: "You have a first meeting with VP of X at Company Y"
- Play the prospect role

**What to Assess:**
- Opening: Do they set an agenda and earn the right to ask questions?
- Discovery: Do they ask open-ended questions and dig deeper?
- Pain: Do they uncover business pain and quantify impact?
- Next steps: Do they propose clear next steps and get commitment?

**Role Play - Objection Handling:**
- "We don't have budget for this."
- "We're already working with Competitor X."
- "I need to think about it."
- "Can you send me some information?"

### AE Red Flags
- Can't articulate their sales process
- Takes credit for team wins without specifics
- Talks more than listens in role play
- Can't explain why deals were lost
- No questions about the product, market, or team

## Sales Manager Interviews

### What to Assess

| Competency | What to Look For |
|------------|------------------|
| Team Leadership | Builds, coaches, retains talent |
| Performance Management | Sets goals, manages accountability |
| Deal Support | Helps reps close, adds value |
| Forecasting | Accurate pipeline management |
| Recruiting | Track record of hiring well |

### Sales Manager Questions

**Team Building:**
- How do you approach hiring? What do you look for?
- Tell me about someone you hired who became a top performer.
- Tell me about someone you had to let go. What happened?
- How do you onboard a new rep?

**Coaching:**
- How do you run 1:1s with your reps?
- Tell me about a rep who was struggling. How did you help them?
- How do you identify when a rep needs coaching vs. when they need to go?
- What's your approach to deal reviews?

**Performance Management:**
- How do you set quotas for your team?
- What do you do when someone consistently misses quota?
- How do you balance pushing for results with rep wellbeing?

**Leadership Scenario:**
- "Your team is at 60% of quota with one month left. What do you do?"
- "Your top rep just got an offer from a competitor. How do you handle it?"
- "A rep complains that the product isn't competitive. How do you respond?"

## VP Sales / CRO Interviews

### What to Assess

| Competency | What to Look For |
|------------|------------------|
| Strategy | GTM vision, market understanding |
| Org Design | Builds effective sales organizations |
| Metrics | Data-driven, understands SaaS economics |
| Executive Presence | Can sell to board, investors, execs |
| Recruiting | Builds leadership teams |

### VP Sales Questions

**Strategy:**
- How would you approach our market? What's your 90-day plan?
- Where do you see the biggest GTM opportunities for us?
- How do you think about segmentation and coverage model?
- What's your philosophy on product-led vs sales-led growth?

**Org Building:**
- Walk me through a sales org you built from scratch.
- How do you decide when to add new roles (SDR, SE, etc.)?
- How do you structure compensation to align with company goals?
- What's your approach to sales operations and enablement?

**Metrics & Forecasting:**
- What metrics do you track to assess sales health?
- How do you build a forecast? What's your accuracy typically?
- How do you think about sales efficiency and productivity?

**Executive Exercise:**
- Have them present a GTM plan for your business
- Assess strategic thinking, communication, and executive presence

## Evaluating Sales Candidates

### Signs of a Strong Candidate
- Prepared (researched company, product, interviewers)
- Curious (asks thoughtful questions throughout)
- Specific (gives concrete examples, not generalities)
- Self-aware (knows strengths and weaknesses)
- Metrics-oriented (tracks and shares their numbers)
- Customer-focused (talks about customer problems, not just features)

### Common Mistakes in Sales Hiring

| Mistake | Why It Happens | How to Avoid |
|---------|----------------|--------------|
| Hiring based on charisma alone | Sales people are good at interviewing | Use structured role plays |
| Not checking numbers | Candidates inflate results | Verify with references, ask for specifics |
| Ignoring culture fit | Desperate to hire | Include team fit interview |
| Hiring for wrong stage | Prior company was different | Probe for stage-appropriate experience |
| Skipping role play | Takes time to set up | Make it mandatory for AE+ roles |

### Reference Check Questions for Sales

- What was their quota attainment over time?
- How did they compare to other reps?
- What was their ramp time?
- How coachable were they?
- Would you hire them again? Why or why not?
- What should I know about managing them?
