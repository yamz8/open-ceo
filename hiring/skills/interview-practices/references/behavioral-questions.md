# Behavioral Question Bank

Organized by competency. Use these as starting points and customize for your role.

## Problem-Solving & Judgment

**Tell me about a time you had to make a decision with incomplete information.**
- Follow-up: What information did you wish you had?
- Follow-up: How did you validate your decision afterward?

**Describe a complex problem you solved. Walk me through your approach.**
- Follow-up: What alternatives did you consider?
- Follow-up: How did you know when you had the right solution?

**Tell me about a time you identified a problem no one else saw.**
- Follow-up: How did you convince others it was worth addressing?
- Follow-up: What was the outcome?

**Describe a situation where you had to change your approach mid-project.**
- Follow-up: What triggered the change?
- Follow-up: How did you manage the transition?

## Communication & Influence

**Tell me about a time you had to explain something complex to someone unfamiliar with the topic.**
- Follow-up: How did you know they understood?
- Follow-up: What would you do differently?

**Describe a situation where you had to get buy-in from stakeholders who disagreed.**
- Follow-up: What resistance did you encounter?
- Follow-up: How did you address concerns?

**Tell me about a time you received feedback that was hard to hear.**
- Follow-up: How did you respond in the moment?
- Follow-up: What did you do with that feedback?

**Describe a time you had to deliver difficult news to someone.**
- Follow-up: How did you prepare?
- Follow-up: How did they react?

## Collaboration & Teamwork

**Tell me about a time you worked with someone whose working style was very different from yours.**
- Follow-up: How did you adapt?
- Follow-up: What did you learn from them?

**Describe a situation where you helped a teammate who was struggling.**
- Follow-up: How did you approach the conversation?
- Follow-up: What was the result?

**Tell me about a conflict with a colleague and how you resolved it.**
- Follow-up: What was at the root of the disagreement?
- Follow-up: How is your relationship now?

**Describe a successful team project you were part of. What was your role?**
- Follow-up: What made the team effective?
- Follow-up: What would you improve about how the team worked?

## Ownership & Initiative

**Tell me about a time you went beyond your job description to get something done.**
- Follow-up: What motivated you?
- Follow-up: How did your manager react?

**Describe a project you initiated without being asked.**
- Follow-up: How did you get support for it?
- Follow-up: What was the impact?

**Tell me about a time you took responsibility for a mistake.**
- Follow-up: How did you address it?
- Follow-up: What did you learn?

**Describe a situation where you saw something that could be improved and took action.**
- Follow-up: What barriers did you face?
- Follow-up: What was the outcome?

## Adaptability & Learning

**Tell me about a time you had to learn something new quickly to complete a project.**
- Follow-up: What resources did you use?
- Follow-up: How did you know you'd learned enough?

**Describe a situation where you had to adapt to a major change.**
- Follow-up: What was most challenging?
- Follow-up: What helped you adapt?

**Tell me about a failure or setback and what you learned from it.**
- Follow-up: What would you do differently?
- Follow-up: How has it changed your approach?

**Describe a time when you received coaching that changed how you work.**
- Follow-up: What specifically changed?
- Follow-up: How did you implement the feedback?

## Customer/User Focus

**Tell me about a time you advocated for the customer/user when others disagreed.**
- Follow-up: What evidence did you use?
- Follow-up: What was the outcome?

**Describe a situation where you learned something surprising from customer feedback.**
- Follow-up: How did you gather that feedback?
- Follow-up: What did you do with that learning?

**Tell me about a time you had to balance user needs with business constraints.**
- Follow-up: How did you make the tradeoff?
- Follow-up: How did you communicate the decision?

## Results & Execution

**Tell me about a time you had to deliver under a tight deadline.**
- Follow-up: How did you prioritize?
- Follow-up: What did you learn about working under pressure?

**Describe your most significant professional accomplishment.**
- Follow-up: What made it significant?
- Follow-up: What obstacles did you overcome?

**Tell me about a goal you didn't achieve. What happened?**
- Follow-up: What did you learn?
- Follow-up: What would you do differently?

**Describe a time you had to juggle multiple priorities.**
- Follow-up: How did you decide what to focus on?
- Follow-up: How did you communicate with stakeholders?

## Using These Questions

1. **Pick 3-4 questions** per interview based on competencies you're assessing
2. **Customize the context** to be relevant to the role
3. **Use follow-ups** to get specific details
4. **Listen for STAR format** (Situation, Task, Action, Result)
5. **Probe vague answers** - "Can you give me a specific example?"

## Red Flags in Responses

- Can't give specific examples (only hypotheticals)
- Takes credit for team accomplishments without acknowledging others
- Blames others for failures without self-reflection
- Answers don't align with resume/experience level
- Overly rehearsed answers without authentic detail
