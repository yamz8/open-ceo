# Sales Compensation Guide

## Sales Compensation Structure

Sales compensation typically combines base salary with variable pay (commission/bonus).

### Common Structures

| Structure | Description | When to Use |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| 50/50 | Half base, half variable | Standard for quota-carrying AEs |
| 60/40 | 60% base, 40% variable | Higher base for enterprise, longer cycles |
| 70/30 | 70% base, 30% variable | SDRs, newer reps, complex sales |

### On-Target Earnings (OTE)

OTE = Base Salary + Variable at 100% quota attainment

**Example:** $80K base + $80K variable = $160K OTE

## Commission Structures

### Simple Commission
- Fixed % of deal value
- Example: 10% of ACV
- Easy to understand, can encourage discounting

### Tiered Commission
- Higher % as quota attainment increases
- Example: 8% at 0-100%, 12% at 100-150%, 15% at 150%+
- Encourages overperformance

### Accelerators
- Multipliers above quota
- Example: 1.5x multiplier above 100%
- Rewards top performers significantly

### Decelerators (rarely used)
- Reduced commission below threshold
- Example: 50% rate below 75% attainment
- Can demotivate struggling reps

## Quota Setting

### Quota to OTE Ratio

| Stage | Typical Ratio | Notes |
|-------|---------------|-------|
| Early (Seed/A) | 3-4x | Lower quotas while finding PMF |
| Growth (B/C) | 4-6x | More established playbook |
| Scale (D+) | 5-8x | Efficient sales machine |

### Ramp Period

| Role | Typical Ramp | Quota During Ramp |
|------|--------------|-------------------|
| SDR | 1-2 months | 50%, then 75%, then 100% |
| AE | 3-4 months | 25%, 50%, 75%, 100% |
| Enterprise AE | 4-6 months | More gradual ramp |

## First Sales Hire Considerations

- Often founder-led sales until $500K-1M ARR
- First AE hire is critical - consider proven operators
- May pay premium (75th percentile) for first sales hire
- Equity should reflect building function from scratch
- Consider player-coach who can eventually manage
